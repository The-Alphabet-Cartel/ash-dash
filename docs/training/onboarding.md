## Welcome to the Team

First, thank you for volunteering your time and emotional energy to support our community. Being a CRT member is meaningful work, and we're glad to have you.

## Onboarding Checklist

### Week 1: Orientation

- [ ] Read the [Crisis Response Guide](/crt/crisis-response-guide)
- [ ] Review the [Self-Care Guide](/crt/self-care)
- [ ] Get access to CRT Discord channels
- [ ] Get access to Ash-Dash dashboard
- [ ] Meet your CRT mentor
- [ ] Attend CRT orientation meeting

### Week 2: Shadowing

- [ ] Shadow experienced CRT members on 3+ sessions
- [ ] Review session notes and documentation practices
- [ ] Practice using Ash-Dash features
- [ ] Discuss observations with your mentor

### Week 3: Supervised Practice

- [ ] Handle your first session with mentor support
- [ ] Debrief with mentor after each session
- [ ] Continue shadowing complex cases
- [ ] Complete training scenarios

### Week 4: Independent Practice

- [ ] Handle sessions independently
- [ ] Know when and how to escalate
- [ ] Regular check-ins with mentor
- [ ] Officially welcomed at CRT meeting! üéâ

## Understanding Ash-Dash

### Key Features

1. **Dashboard**
   - View active crisis sessions
   - Monitor community health metrics
   - See CRT activity

2. **Sessions**
   - Access session details
   - View user message history
   - Read Ash's analysis
   - Write session notes

3. **Documentation**
   - Training materials (you're here!)
   - CRT protocols
   - Reference guides

### Session Workflow

```
Alert ‚Üí Review ‚Üí Respond ‚Üí Document ‚Üí Close
```

1. **Alert**: Ash detects potential crisis
2. **Review**: Check context and history
3. **Respond**: Reach out to community member
4. **Document**: Write session notes
5. **Close**: Mark session as resolved

## Communication Guidelines

### Do's

- Use warm, compassionate language
- Listen actively and validate feelings
- Ask open-ended questions
- Respect boundaries and privacy
- Document all interactions

### Don'ts

- Don't minimize or dismiss feelings
- Don't make promises you can't keep
- Don't share details without consent
- Don't diagnose or prescribe
- Don't go it alone on difficult cases

## When to Escalate

Escalate to CRT Leadership when:

- Immediate danger to self or others
- You feel out of your depth
- The situation involves minors
- Legal concerns arise
- You need support

**It's always okay to ask for help.**

## Taking Care of Yourself

This work can be heavy. Remember:

- You're not responsible for others' choices
- Setting boundaries is healthy
- Taking breaks is encouraged
- Your wellbeing matters

Read the full [Self-Care Guide](/crt/self-care) for more.
